# PART 1: BEFORE YOU START

## 1.1 Understand the Erasmus Mundus System

- **There is NO central application portal.** Each program has its own website and application system.
- **Applying to the program = applying for the scholarship.** You don't submit a separate scholarship form.
- **You can apply to MAXIMUM 3 programs** per cycle (this is an EU rule).
- **Applications are FREE.** No application fees for any Erasmus Mundus program.
- **Deadlines are strict.** Missing by even 1 minute = disqualified.

# PART 2: DOCUMENTS YOU NEED

## 2.1 Common Documents (Required by ALL Programs)

### A. Bachelor's Degree Certificate
- Get a **certified copy** from Dhaka College
- If not in English: get a **certified English translation**
- Some programs accept a **provisional certificate** if you haven't graduated yet

### B. Academic Transcripts (Full Record)
- Request **official transcripts** from Dhaka College Registrar
- Must list ALL courses taken and grades received
- Get **certified English translation** if original is in Bengali
- Must show CGPA: 3.25/4.00

### C. Europass CV
- Use the official format: https://europa.eu/europass/en
- Include:
  - Personal info + contact
  - Education: BSc Statistics, Dhaka College (2022–ongoing)
  - Work experience: Aspirant Machine Learning Engineer
  - Projects: AgriTech, VocabPro, StatWise, Heart Disease Prediction, Spam SMS Detection
  - Technical skills: Python, Scikit-learn, TensorFlow, PyTorch, FastAPI, Streamlit
  - Languages: Bengali (Native), English (Professional)
  - Leadership: Project Manager (Data Analytics Club), Volunteer for Bangladesh, YSSE

### D. Motivation Letter (Statement of Purpose)
- **1 per program** (do NOT reuse the same letter)
- Length: 500–1000 words (varies by program)
- Structure:
  - **Paragraph 1:** Who you are, what you want to study, why this specific program
  - **Paragraph 2:** Your academic background (Statistics) and how it prepares you
  - **Paragraph 3:** Your professional experience (ML Engineer) and projects
  - **Paragraph 4:** Why this specific program + consortium (mention specific universities)
  - **Paragraph 5:** Your future goals and how you'll contribute
- **For EMAI:** Emphasize ML projects, Deep Learning, AI interest
- **For InterMaths:** Emphasize Statistics foundation, mathematical modelling, stochastic interest
- **For DEAI:** Emphasize data engineering, ML deployment, FastAPI/Streamlit experience

### E. English Language Proficiency
- **IELTS Academic** is the most widely accepted
- Target: **6.5 overall** (most programs require B2 = 6.0, but 6.5 is safer)
- Test validity: 2 years
- Book your test at British Council Dhaka or IDP Dhaka
- Some programs accept TOEFL iBT 80+ or Cambridge B2 First 173+
- **Exemption:** If your Bachelor's was taught entirely in English, some programs waive this

### F. Recommendation Letters (2–3)
- **Academic recommender:** 1 Statistics professor from Dhaka College
- **Professional recommender:** 1 supervisor from your ML Engineer job
- **Optional:** 1 from volunteer organization (for AISS program)
- **How to ask:** Email them with your CV, the program details, and deadlines. Give them 4–6 weeks.
- Programs may email them directly or you upload signed letters

### G. Passport Copy
- Valid passport (must not expire before Sep 2028)
- Color scan of the bio-data page

## 2.2 Program-Specific Extra Documents

### For EDISS (if you apply):
- **Video answer:** 2–3 minute video responding to a prompt (e.g., "Why EDISS and how does your background fit?")
  - Upload as unlisted YouTube link or file
  - Dress professionally, good lighting, clear audio
  - Speak clearly about your ML projects and interest in data-intensive software

### For AISS (if you apply):
- **Situated motivation video:** 2–3 min video explaining a specific problem AI can solve
  - Upload as unlisted YouTube/Vimeo link
  - Talk about your AgriTech project and Bangladeshi agriculture challenges
- **Portfolio:** PDF showcasing your best projects (AgriTech, StatWise, VocabPro)
  - Include screenshots, architecture diagrams, results
  - Max 10 pages
- **Self-funding plan:** 1-page PDF explaining how you'd fund yourself if no scholarship
  - Be realistic: savings, family support, part-time work, etc.

### For MATHS-DISC (if you apply):
- **Study plan:** Your preferred partner institutions for each semester
- **CGPA proof:** Must be >75% (check what 3.25/4.00 equals in percentage)
- **3 mathematical background courses** you'd like to be interviewed on

## Phase 3: Results & Acceptance (March – August 2027)

### March–April 2027
- [ ] **Interviews:** If shortlisted for AISS or EMAI, you'll get an interview invite
  - AISS: 15 min (10 min presentation + 5 min Q&A)
  - EMAI: May interview shortlisted candidates
- [ ] Check email **every day** (including spam folder)
- [ ] Respond to any requests **within 24 hours**

### April–June 2027
- [ ] **Results start arriving:**
  - Main list: you get a scholarship offer
  - Reserve list: you may be contacted later (up to September)
  - Rejected: move to backup plans
- [ ] If offered a scholarship:
  - **Accept within 3–5 days** (strict deadline)
  - Sign the scholarship agreement
  - You'll get instructions for enrollment

### June–July 2027
- [ ] **Visa application:**
  - Which country? Your first host country:
    - EMAI → Spain (Barcelona)
    - InterMaths → Italy (L'Aquila)
    - DEAI → Belgium (Brussels)
  - Go to the embassy/consulate in Dhaka
  - Documents needed:
    - Valid passport
    - Admission letter
    - Scholarship award letter
    - Proof of accommodation
    - Travel health insurance
    - Visa application form
    - Passport photos
    - Financial proof (scholarship letter covers this)
  - Processing time: 4–8 weeks (apply EARLY)
- [ ] **Arrange accommodation** (university dorm or private)
- [ ] **Book flights** (the scholarship will reimburse)

### August 2027
- [ ] Attend **online pre-departure orientation** (program will arrange)
- [ ] Pack:
  - Documents (originals + copies)
  - Laptop, electronics
  - Clothes for all seasons
  - Medications
  - **Pack light** — you'll move to another country in 4–6 months!
- [ ] Notify your current employer

# PART 6: COMMON MISTAKES TO AVOID

| Mistake | Solution |
|---------|----------|
| Applying to more than 3 programs | Stick to 3 max — EU rule |
| Submitting on the last day | Submit 48+ hours before deadline |
| Using the same motivation letter | Write 1 unique letter per program |
| Wrong document format | Use PDF only (unless specified) |
| Missing program-specific requirements | Read the "Required Documents" page on each website |
| Forgetting to upload a document | Use a checklist before final submit |
| Not checking spam folder | Check spam daily — interview invites go there |
| Missing the acceptance deadline | Accept within 3–5 days of receiving offer |
| Not preparing for interviews | Practice explaining your projects and motivation |
| Not having backup plans | Also apply to DAAD, Chevening, MBZUAI, Fulbright |
