# Motivation Letter Template — Fully Funded Scholarships 2027

## Structure Overview

A winning motivation letter follows this structure:

| Section | Length | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| **Opening** | 1 paragraph | Hook: who you are, what you're applying for, why this specific programme |
| **Academic Background** | 1-2 paragraphs | Your BSc Statistics, key courses, projects. Connect to programme requirements |
| **Professional Experience** | 1-2 paragraphs | Your ML Engineer role, projects (VocabPro, StatWise, AgriTech). Evidence of skills |
| **Why This Programme** | 1-2 paragraphs | Specific tracks, professors, host universities, courses — must be tailored |
| **Future Goals** | 1 paragraph | Short-term (Master) + long-term (PhD / career) + how you'll give back |
| **Closing** | 1 paragraph | Summary + confidence in contribution |

## Before You Start — Research Checklist

- [ ] Visit the programme website — read the full curriculum
- [ ] Identify 2-3 specific courses that match your background
- [ ] Identify 1-2 professors whose research interests you
- [ ] Note the specialisation tracks available
- [ ] Check the host universities — pick your preferred track
- [ ] Note any unique features (summer schools, internships, industry partners)

## Full Template

### Opening Paragraph

> Dear Selection Committee,
>
> I am writing to express my strong interest in the [PROGRAMME NAME] for the [YEAR] intake. As an Aspirant Machine Learning Engineer with a BSc in Statistics from the University of Dhaka, I have developed a solid foundation in [mathematical modelling / statistical learning / data analysis] and practical expertise in building production-ready AI systems. [PROGRAMME NAME]'s unique combination of [SPECIFIC FEATURE 1] and [SPECIFIC FEATURE 2] aligns perfectly with my academic background and professional aspirations.

### Academic Background

> My academic journey began with a BSc in Statistics from Dhaka College, University of Dhaka (CGPA 3.25/4.00), where I built a strong quantitative foundation. My coursework included [Probability Theory, Mathematical Statistics, Regression Analysis, Time Series Analysis, Multivariate Analysis, Sampling Techniques], giving me rigorous training in [statistical modelling / data analysis / mathematical reasoning].
>
> For my thesis/project, I [describe final year project]. This experience taught me [key skill] and sparked my interest in [area].

### Professional Experience

> Professionally, I work as an Aspirant Machine Learning Engineer, where I design and deploy ML solutions in production environments. My experience spans the full ML lifecycle — from data collection and feature engineering to model training, evaluation, and API deployment.
>
> Key projects include:
> - **VocabPro SaaS** — A vocabulary learning platform built with FastAPI, PostgreSQL, and Streamlit. I designed the database architecture, implemented the REST API, and deployed on Render. This taught me end-to-end software engineering for AI applications.
> - **StatWise** — A statistical analysis web application using Python, Streamlit, Pandas, and Scikit-learn. I implemented data preprocessing, statistical testing, and interactive visualisation.
> - **AgriTech Crop Recommendation** — An ML system recommending optimal crops based on soil parameters. I trained classification models achieving [X]% accuracy and deployed via FastAPI.

### Why This Programme

> [THIS IS THE MOST IMPORTANT SECTION — MUST BE TAILORED]
>
> [PROGRAMME NAME] stands out to me because of its [SPECIFIC FEATURE]. The [TRACK NAME] specialisation at [HOST UNIVERSITY] directly aligns with my goal to [CAREER GOAL]. I am particularly excited about the course "[COURSE NAME]" taught by Professor [NAME], as it covers [TOPIC] which is central to my research interests.

> I am confident that my combination of statistical training, practical ML engineering experience, and passion for [FIELD] makes me a strong candidate for [PROGRAMME NAME]. I am eager to contribute to and learn from the diverse community of scholars in this programme. Thank you for considering my application.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- **Generic letters** — Sending the same letter to every programme. Each consortium has unique features — mention them.
- **Too technical** — Show passion and motivation, not just a list of skills.
- **Ignoring the "why"** — Explain why this specific programme, not just why you want a Master's.
- **Weak opening** — First paragraph determines whether they keep reading. Start strong.
- **No future plan** — Committees want to know their investment will yield results. Show your trajectory.
- **Exceeding limits** — Respect word/character/video length limits strictly.
