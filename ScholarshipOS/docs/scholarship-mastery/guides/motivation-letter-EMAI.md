## Full Draft

Dear Selection Committee,

I am writing to express my strong interest in the Erasmus Mundus Joint Master in Artificial Intelligence (EMAI) for the 2027 intake. As an Aspirant Machine Learning Engineer with a BSc in Statistics from the University of Dhaka, I have developed a solid foundation in probabilistic reasoning and mathematical modelling, and practical expertise in building production-ready ML systems. EMAI's unique combination of symbolic and statistical AI, delivered across four leading European universities, aligns perfectly with my academic background and professional aspirations.

My academic journey began with a BSc in Statistics from Dhaka College, University of Dhaka (CGPA 3.25/4.00), where I built a rigorous quantitative foundation. My coursework included Probability Theory, Mathematical Statistics, Regression Analysis, Time Series Analysis, Multivariate Analysis, and Sampling Techniques — all of which gave me deep training in statistical modelling and data-driven reasoning. The mathematical rigour of my degree, particularly in probability theory and stochastic processes, has proven invaluable in understanding the theoretical underpinnings of machine learning algorithms, from Bayesian inference to ensemble methods.

Professionally, I work as an Aspirant Machine Learning Engineer, where I design, train, and deploy ML solutions in production environments. My experience spans the full ML lifecycle — data collection and feature engineering, model training and evaluation, API development, and cloud deployment — giving me practical insight into the end-to-end challenges of building intelligent systems.

Three key projects define my professional work. First, **VocabPro**, a SaaS vocabulary learning platform built with FastAPI, PostgreSQL, and Streamlit. I designed the database architecture, implemented RESTful APIs, deployed on Render, and integrated ML-based spaced repetition algorithms for personalised learning. This project taught me the software engineering discipline required to ship reliable AI applications. Second, **StatWise**, a statistical analysis web application built with Python, Streamlit, Pandas, and Scikit-learn, featuring interactive data visualisation and statistical testing. Third, an **AgriTech crop recommendation system** that uses soil parameter data to recommend optimal crops, achieving strong classification accuracy through ensemble methods and deployed via FastAPI. This last project, in particular, demonstrated to me the power of AI to solve real-world problems in agriculture — a sector critical to Bangladesh's economy.

EMAI stands out to me because of its distinctive integration of symbolic and statistical AI — a synthesis I believe is essential for building the next generation of explainable, trustworthy intelligent systems. The Data Science specialisation track at the University of Ljubljana directly aligns with my background in Statistics and my goal to deepen my expertise at the intersection of probabilistic modelling and machine learning. I am particularly excited about the Bayesian Machine Learning and Probabilistic Graphical Models courses, which build naturally on my Statistics training. The Intelligent Decision Making track at Sapienza University of Rome is equally compelling, offering a path into reinforcement learning and decision theory that connects my quantitative foundation to cutting-edge AI research.

The UCL London AI Summer School, a unique component of the EMAI programme, represents an unparalleled opportunity to engage with world-class AI research and connect with peers from across the programme. Furthermore, the multi-university structure — studying at UPF Barcelona, a partner specialisation university, and completing a master's thesis across the network — provides the breadth of exposure I seek as I prepare for a research career in machine learning.

EMAIM's commitment to diversity, with 37 nationalities represented across 79 students, is particularly meaningful to me as a Bangladeshi applicant. I would bring a perspective shaped by working on AI solutions in a developing country context, where resource constraints and unique societal challenges demand creative, practical approaches to AI deployment.

In the short term, I aim to deepen my expertise in probabilistic machine learning and decision intelligence through EMAI's curriculum and thesis research. In the long term, I plan to pursue a PhD in machine learning at a leading European or North American university, contributing to research on interpretable and robust AI systems. Ultimately, I aspire to return to academia or research, working on AI solutions that address challenges in the Global South — from agricultural optimisation to healthcare access — leveraging the skills and network I would build through this programme.

I am confident that my combination of statistical training, practical ML engineering experience, and passion for AI research makes me a strong candidate for the EMAI programme. I am eager to contribute to and learn from the diverse community of scholars that defines this consortium. Thank you for considering my application.

Sincerely,
