## Eligibility
- Bachelor's in **CS, Software Engineering, or equivalent** with coursework in:
  - Programming, algorithms, data structures
  - Basic computer architecture
  - Computer networks
  - Software engineering
  - **Mathematics** (your Statistics degree covers this)
- Relevant **work experience** in CS/software is considered
- English proficiency (IELTS 6.0+ typically)
- **Your ML Engineer role counts as relevant work experience**

## Application Process

### Step 1 — Check Eligibility (Jun–Sep 2026)
Review if your coursework covers the CS fundamentals. Your Statistics degree + ML coursework + job experience creates a strong case.

### Step 2 — Prepare Documents (Sep–Nov 2026)
- **Online application form** (on Studyinfo.fi portal)
- **CV** in Europass format
- **Motivation letter** (tailored to EDISS — emphasize ML+software integration)
- **Transcripts + degree** (certified, in color PDF)
- **English proficiency** certificate
- **Video answer** (2–3 min — respond to a prompt about your interest in the program)
- **2 recommendation letters**
- **Passport copy**

### Step 3 — Submit Video + Application (Nov 2026 – Jan 2027)
- The EDISS application requires a **video answer** — prepare this in advance
- Application round opens early November, closes early January
- Note: application must be completed in one sitting (no save-draft)

### Step 4 — Evaluation (Jan–Mar 2027)
- Academic merit, motivation, video quality, recommendations assessed
- Highly competitive (1,133 applicants for ~15-20 scholarships)

### Step 5 — Results (Mar–May 2027)
- Main list and reserve list notified

### Step 6 — Visa & Pre-departure (May–Aug 2027)
- Finnish student visa (first country: Turku)
- Accommodation arrangement

### Step 7 — Start (Aug/Sep 2027)
- Program begins in Turku, Finland
