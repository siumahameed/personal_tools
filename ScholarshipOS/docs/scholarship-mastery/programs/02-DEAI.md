# DEAI — Erasmus Mundus Joint Master in Data Engineering and Artificial Intelligence

## Overview
A 2-year (120 ECTS) program training experts in **large-scale data engineering, analytics, and AI**. Successor to the renowned BDMA (Big Data Management and Analytics) program.

## Eligibility
- Bachelor's degree (≥180 ECTS) in **CS, Mathematics, or closely related field**
- Strong foundation in:
  - Relational databases
  - Algorithms & data structures
  - **Probability & statistics**
  - Graph theory / set theory / logic
  - Object-oriented programming
- English proficiency: B2 level minimum (IELTS 6.0+)
- **Your Statistics degree with programming skills satisfies all requirements**

## Application Process

### Step 1 — Check Requirement (Jun–Sep 2026)
Verify your bachelor's covers the prerequisites. Your Statistics + ML coursework should be sufficient.

### Step 2 — Prepare Documents (Sep–Nov 2026)
- **Europass CV** highlighting ML engineering and data projects
- **Motivation letter** (1–2 pages, specific to DEAI)
- **Certified transcripts** + degree certificate (English translation)
- **English proficiency** (IELTS 6.0+ / TOEFL 80+)
- **2 reference letters** (ideally 1 academic + 1 professional)
- **Passport copy**

### Step 3 — Submit Application (Oct 2026 – Jan 2027)
- Apply on the DEAI online portal: https://deai.ulb.be/
- Scholarship deadline for non-EU: typically **mid-January**
- Self-funded round opens later (March–June) if needed

### Step 4 — Selection (Feb–Apr 2027)
- Scoring: bachelor domain (CS credit weight), work experience, cover letter quality
- No interview typically — purely document-based evaluation

### Step 5 — Results (Apr–Jun 2027)
- Main list submitted to European Commission for approval
- Reserve list candidates may wait until September

### Step 6 — Visa & Travel (Jun–Aug 2027)
- Belgian student visa (first country: Brussels)
- Arrange accommodation, travel, insurance

### Step 7 — Start (Sep 2027)
- First semester at ULB, Brussels
