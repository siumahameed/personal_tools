# COSSE — Computer Simulations for Science and Engineering

## Overview
A 2-year (120 ECTS) **Erasmus Mundus double degree** master in Computational Science and Engineering (CSE) — the "third pillar of science" alongside theory and experiment. Delivered by 3 top European technical universities.

## Consortium Universities
| Year | University | Location |
|------|-----------|----------|
| Year 1 | Technical University of Berlin (TU Berlin) | Berlin, Germany |
| Year 2 | KTH Royal Institute of Technology **OR** TU Delft | Stockholm, Sweden / Delft, Netherlands |

You receive **double degrees** from both universities.

## Why It Fits You
- Open to **Mathematics, Engineering, Natural Sciences** graduates
- Includes **Machine Learning**, data assimilation, numerical analysis, HPC
- **66% of graduates go on to PhD** — strong research pathway
- Triple university network: TU Berlin, KTH, TU Delft
- Well-established program (since 2010)
- Your Statistics + programming background is a strong foundation

## Eligibility
- BSc in **Mathematics, Engineering, or Natural Sciences** with strong mathematics
- Good English proficiency
- Apply via universityadmissions.se (Swedish central system)

## Application Process
1. Apply via https://www.kth.se/en/studies/master/computer-simulations-for-science-and-engineering
2. Submit through universityadmissions.se (Swedish central application)
3. Deadline: ~15 January
4. Results: ~April
