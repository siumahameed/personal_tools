# AISS — Artificial Intelligence for Sustainable Societies

## Overview
A 2-year (120 ECTS) Erasmus Mundus joint master's applying **AI to real-world sustainability challenges**. Interdisciplinary focus combining technical AI skills with societal impact.

## Consortium Universities
| Year | University | Location |
|------|-----------|----------|
| Year 1, Sem 1 | Tallinn University | Tallinn, Estonia |
| Year 1, Sem 2 | Lusófona University | Lisbon, Portugal |
| Year 2, Sem 3 | Tampere University | Tampere, Finland |
| Year 2, Sem 4 | Choice of location | Thesis / internship anywhere |

## Why It Fits You
- AI-focused curriculum matches your ML Engineer experience
- Your **AgriTech** project (AI for Bangladeshi farmers) is a **perfect example** of AI for sustainable societies
- Your volunteer work (Volunteer for Bangladesh) shows commitment to social impact
- Smaller scholarship pool (10/year) but less competition from pure-CS applicants

## Scholarship
| Item | Amount |
|------|--------|
| Monthly stipend | €1,400 (24 months = €33,600) |
| Tuition fees | Fully covered (normally €2,000/semester = €8,000 total) |
| Travel + insurance | Covered |
| **Only 10 scholarships per intake** | Highly competitive, but focus on social impact sets it apart |

## Eligibility
- Bachelor's degree in **any relevant field** (CS, Mathematics, Statistics, Engineering, Social Sciences)
- **Situated motivation video** (2–3 min — unique to AISS)
- **Portfolio** demonstrating AI/technical skillset
- **Self-funding plan** (explain how you'd fund yourself if not selected for scholarship)
- English proficiency
- Interview with admission committee (shortlisted candidates)

## Application Process

### Step 1 — Understand the Unique Requirements (Jun–Sep 2026)
AISS requires extra materials: a **motivation video**, a **portfolio**, and a **self-funding plan**. Start brainstorming early.

### Step 2 — Prepare Documents (Sep–Nov 2026)
- **CV** emphasizing AI projects AND social impact
- **Motivation letter** — connect your AI work to sustainability/SDGs
- **Situated motivation video** (2–3 min) — upload as unlisted YouTube/Vimeo link
  - Talk about a specific problem you've observed and how AI can address it
- **Portfolio** — PDF showcasing your ML projects (include AgriTech, StatWise)
- **Self-funding plan** — 1-page document explaining backup funding strategy
- **Transcripts + degree certificate**
- **English proficiency** certificate
- **2 recommendation letters**
- **Passport copy**

### Step 3 — Submit Application (Nov 2026 – Feb 2027)
- Apply via DreamApply (Tallinn University's system)
- Deadline typically early February

### Step 4 — Interview (Feb–Mar 2027)
- Shortlisted candidates invited for 15-min online interview
- 10 min presentation + 5 min Q&A
- Prepare to discuss your motivation video and portfolio

### Step 5 — Results (Mar–May 2027)
- Top 10 ranked candidates receive scholarship
- Reserve list may be contacted later

### Step 6 — Visa & Pre-departure (May–Aug 2027)
- Estonian student visa (first country: Tallinn)
- Plan finances for initial weeks before first scholarship payment (October)

### Step 7 — Start (Sep 2027)
- Program begins in Tallinn, Estonia
